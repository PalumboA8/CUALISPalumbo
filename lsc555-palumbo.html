Adam Palumbo's Biography

Early Life

Adam was born on June 8th, 2000 in Teaneck, New Jersey. He spent the first 18 years of his life in Oakland, New Jersey. In High School, he played the following sports:

  • soccer
  • swimming
  • bowling

    • He attended Flagler College in St. Augustine, Florida where he graduated with a Bachelor's in History. As part of his degree, he worked with Marineland of Florida and the Florida National Guard Headquarters Museum.

    After initially wanting to work as a curator, Adam fell into the archival career after the working with the Florida National Guard. After graduating college in May 2022, Adam moved to Arlington, VA to work as an intern at the Center for Local History (CLH). In this position, he created local history blog posts such as THIS one on Orville Wright in Arlington.

    After leaving the CLH, Adam moved to the Miami, Florida, to work as an intern in the South Florida Collections Management Center for the National Parks Service. Here he worked on archival collections for all 5 major South Florida National Parks, including the Everglades and Biscayne National Park.

    When the internship at the Everglades ended, Adam moved back to Arlington, VA to finish his MLIS at Catholic University. He is currently working with both Catholic University and the National Library of Medicine as an archivist.
